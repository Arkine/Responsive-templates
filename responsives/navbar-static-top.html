Progressive Growth of Preferred Resources

California prides itself on being a national leader in advancing a sustainable energy future. And with good reason. In recent years, California enacted policies aimed at reducing greenhouse gases and increasing renewable resource development. As a result of these forward-thinking policies, California now ranks first in the United States for total megawatts of installed solar and second behind Texas for total megawatts of installed wind

The growth in these preferred resources is nothing short of phenomenal.

In support of the state's goals, the California Independent System Operator (ISO) has steadily integrated more than XX megawatts of zer0-emission resources onto the grid despite the operational challenges inherent with variable resources. More importantly, actual production levels of wind and solar are soaring.

Wind and solar records:

Combined, wind and solar resources now account for 11,121 MW interconnected to the ISO grid. In total, all renewables (including geothermal) make up about 15,000 MW of ISO generation mix.

With these impressive wind and solar production levels, the ISO is advancing a sustainable energy future to further California's goal of generating one-third of the state's electricity using renewable resources by 2020.
