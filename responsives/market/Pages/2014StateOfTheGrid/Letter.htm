CEO Letter

A phenomenal shift has taken place over the past five years to repower our nation’s power grid with wind, solar and other zero-emission resources. The power industry in the west is at the forefront of this profound transformation toward a cleaner, more sustainable energy machine that recognizes society’s preference for clean power and the innovative approaches needed to integrate unprecedented levels of renewable resources.

At the California Independent System Operator Corporation (ISO), we are busy meeting consumers’ need for power while simultaneously addressing climate change.

Through efforts that included revamping the grid planning process to be more holistic, the ISO is finding more effective ways to use flexible resources that start and stop, and ramp up and down very quickly. California now leads the nation in solar output and is second only to Texas in wind integration. In addition, a critical infrastructure modernization effort is bringing greater reliability and stability to the Southern California electric network.

But there’s more to be done. The flexible, bi-directional nature of zero-emission power sources, energy storage and microgrids are clearly changing the power system in many ways, and we remain firmly committed to guiding electric vehicle integration into the power grid. Such grid modernization supports California Governor Edmund G. Brown Jr.’s 2012 executive order to get 1.5 million zero-emission vehicles on California roads by 2025 as well as supporting the utilities in their work to meet the state’s 2020 renewables portfolio standard that calls for 33 percent of retail power sales to be served by clean, green resources.

Through several pilot projects that now include energy storage devices, the ISO and its industry and regional partners are creating new, smart grid infrastructure and rules to optimize these emerging resources on a wider scale as well as creating greater market efficiencies.

The long-lasting benefits of a greener grid are far reaching. The ISO continues to develop the market and operations applications and devices that better monitor the real-time grid. Expanding the ISO real-time energy imbalance market in November will give western utilities and generators added operational flexibility to orchestrate an increasingly green and efficient regional network and lower costs to consumers.

We are building a smarter, stronger power grid that’s safe, secure, reliable and sustainable. We look forward to working with our industry partners, regulators, policymakers and the public across the west as we power the way toward cleaner energy future.
