Business Efficiency

Delivering effective competition

Competitive Market Performance

The ISO markets continued to perform efficiently in 2013, with overall wholesale energy prices remaining highly competitive despite a 30 percent increase in natural gas prices over the previous year.

Natural gas generators supplied about 40 percent of ISO energy in 2013, up from 39 percent in 2012 and 28 percent in 2011. The increase is largely due to the retirement of the 2,200 MW San Onofre Nuclear Generating Station and lower than normal hydroelectric levels.

Without the gas price increase, wholesale electric costs increased by 5 percent, primarily because the state implemented a greenhouse gas cap-and-trade program. Over the course of 2013, prices were highly competitive and averaged very close to what the ISO’s independent Department of Market Monitoring estimated would result under highly competitive conditions. Another factor nudging prices higher in 2013 was a decrease in hydroelectric generation.

Benefitting from renewable generation

About 2,000 MW of peak generating capacity from mostly solar renewable generation was added in 2013. Wind and solar energy interconnected to the ISO grid provided about 8 percent of system energy, compared with about 5 percent in 2012.

Energy from new wind and solar resources is expected to increase at a much higher rate in the next few years as projects to meet the state’s renewables portfolio standard are built. This increases the need for flexible and fast-ramping resources, such as new combined cycle natural gas, demand response and, in time, energy storage, to support the increasing amounts of variable energy. The ISO and the California Public Utilities Commission are working closely to develop the rules and requirements needed for flexible resources that support renewable generators.

The report noted that several ISO market improvement initiatives will create greater market efficiencies including the 15-minute real-time scheduling option for all internal generation and inter-tie transactions, software updates to add a flexible ramping product, and expansion of the network model to include other balancing areas, which went live on October 1, 2014. Each of these improvements enhances the ISO’s ability to dispatch flexible and fast-ramping resources efficiently and reliably.

For more information on the ISO’s competitive wholesale markets, see the independent Department of Market Monitoring 2013 Annual Report on Market Issues and Performance (PDF 12MB). The 2014 annual report is expected in early spring 2015.
